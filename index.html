Welcome to Virginia Industries for the Blind

Twenty years ago Virginia Industries for the Blind (VIB) provided employment opportunities to blind Virginians in two cities, Richmond and Charlottesville. We became synonymous with a workshop culture, most notably providing mattresses and pens to our government customers. A lot has changed over the past two decades. Today, VIB is still creating opportunities for blind Virginians in Richmond and Charlottesville, but we now provide services and operations in Alexandria, Arlington, Crystal City, Falls Church, Hampton, Newport News, Norfolk, Petersburg, Rosslyn, Salem and Virginia Beach. VIB has expanded beyond our manufacturing roots with skilled, trained people in retail operations, postal functions, switchboard operations, kitting, contract management and court debt collections. Our Charlottesville facility is ISO 9001 registered and we are expanding this internationally recognized quality management system to other VIB operations.

VIB has always been about jobs, but we now have a much wider array of employment options, providing pathways to exciting career opportunities. Our mission, which is “to employ and develop Virginians who are blind, visually impaired or deafblind to achieve their desired level of employment through the manufacture and delivery of quality products and services”, focuses on the two most important aspects of our agency; our customers and our 150 plus employees. As part of the Enterprise Division of the Virginia Department for the Blind and Vision Impaired (DBVI) we are also in a unique position to assist in providing a number of vocational services as well.

This website provides detailed information about VIB’s products, services, employees and other helpful information. You may contact us by telephone through our toll free number, 855.842.7867 or our switchboard at 434.295.5168. You may also reach us by following the "contact us" link on this website.

Our Mission

To employ and develop Virginians who are blind, visually impaired or deaf/blind to achieve their desired level of employment through the manufacture and delivery of quality products and services.

About Us

Established almost nine decades ago, VIB started as a private organization in Charlottesville, Virginia through the efforts of the Virginia Association of Workers for the Blind. Now as part of the Enterprise Division of the Virginia Department for the Blind and Vision Impaired (DBVI), VIB works in coordination with the DBVI Services Division and the Virginia Rehabilitation Center for the Blind and Vision Impaired (VRCBVI) to provide a full range of employment, training, and other vocational services to blind individuals across the Commonwealth.

VIB currently has operations in twenty-one locations throughout Virginia, employing more than 150 people, 100+ of whom are blind or visually impaired.

VIB operates two manufacturing facilities, one in Charlottesville and another in Richmond. VIB manufactures U.S. Navy mattresses, University and institutional mattresses, pillows, reflective safety vests, mop heads, mop handles, mop buckets, fire extinguishers, hand sanitizer products, writing instruments, examination gloves, spices and a variety of other products.

VIB’s Service Division provides a variety of employment opportunities in support of both state and federal government customers. We are currently providing services at the Defense Contract Management Agency (DCMA) stock room, U.S. Army Contract Management Support (CMS) on-site at Defense Logistics Agency Headquarters on Fort Belvoir and off-site at VIB’s Richmond manufacturing facility, Virginia’s Court Debt Collection Office, Langley Air Force Base Mail Room, Veterans Administration Medical Center Switchboard Center in Salem, Kitting operations in Virginia Beach and retail support operations at the Super Servmart store on the Norfolk Naval Base.

Our Retail Division is comprised of eleven Base Supply Centers (BSC’s) located at Defense Supply Center - Richmond, in the Pentagon, Fort Lee, Fort Eustis, Fort Belvoir, Mark Center Federal Building, Oceana Naval Air Station, Langley Air Force Base, Hoffman Office complex in Alexandria and the Taylor Building in Crystal City. We also provide our customers with an online, E-Commerce option for their office supply and general consumable needs.

VIB is an affiliate of the National Industries for the Blind (NIB) under the Javits-Wagner-O’Day Act (JWOD) program now known as the AbilityOne program and is an authorized distributor for and manufacturer of AbilityOne products and services. The VIB Charlottesville and Richmond facilities are both certified/registered to ISO 9001:2008 which is a global quality management standard. VIB is also exploring implementing this quality management system in other VIB locations and operations. VIB is self-funded and self-supporting; operating without the use of general funds.

A Brief History

1922
On March 23, 1922 the Virginia General Assembly passed H.B. 117, an act creating the Virginia Commission for the Blind.
1925
The Virginia Association of Workers for the Blind raised the funds necessary and established the Virginia Workshop for the Blind in a temporary location on South Fourth Street, East, Charlottesville. The workshop opened its doors for the first time on August 5, 1925 with an emphasis on training blind men to make brooms, mattresses, weave willow baskets and cane chairs.
1931
Through private donations and subscriptions and on a 1.45 acre site a three story factory was constructed on Monticello Road and opened on January 1. Since then it has undergone numerous expansions and upgrades, the last of which was completed in 2001.
1934
By a special act of the Virginia General Assembly, the Charlottesville facility was purchased by the state from the Virginia Association of Workers for the Blind.
1938
President Franklin D. Roosevelt signs the Wagner-O'Day Act which provides an opportunity to sell blind made products to the Federal Government.
1939
An end of Fiscal Year 1939 (June 30) Production Report shows 2,703 mattresses were made and renovated; 16 box springs made or re-upholstered; 26 wrestling mats made or reconditioned; 1,104 chairs re-seated; 382 pillows and cushions made or reconditioned; 1,985 dozen brooms, 16 door mats and 210 willow baskets produced.
1947
The Virginia Industries for the Blind (VIB) establishes a training center in Richmond.
1952
NIB launches SKILCRAFT, the brand name for quality consumer products made by people who are blind.
1969
VIB completes construction of its second manufacturing facility, this one in Richmond on High Street.
1971
Senator Jacob K. Javits sponsors legislation adding the purchase of services and including agencies serving people with other severe disabilities to the original Wagner-O'Day Act. The legislation becomes known as the Javits-Wagner-O'Day Act (JWOD).
1973
July 1, 1973, the name "Workshop for the Blind" was officially changed to Virginia Industries for the Blind (VIB).
1985
Virginia General Assembly withdraws state financial support, making VIB a self-supporting agency, operating from the revenues generated from the sale of products manufactured and services provided.
1994
December marked the start of the first Service operation provided by VIB. A Customer Service (order taking) department was established at the General Services Administration (GSA) Franconia Supply Center in Springfield Virginia.
1995
VIB is certified for association with National Industries for the Blind (NIB) programs.
1996
Virginia Department of Taxation and VIB team up to provide a Court Debt Collection service.
1998
VIB opened its first two Base Supply Centers (the sponsoring agency was the Defense Supply Service Washington (DSSW)) in Rosslyn and the Skyline Building, Crystal City, Virginia.
VIB begins providing contracted Retail operations support services, first at the Norfolk Navy Base Super ServMart and then expands to provide similar support operations at other DoD agencies.
1999
Langley Air Force Base Postal Center contract is awarded to VIB to provide services entailing receiving, processing, distributing mail and directorizing mail; base locator services; managing receptacles; and providing customer services.
2000
VIB opens an Individual Equipment Element (IEE) and Base Supply Center retail store at Langley Air Force Base, Hampton, Virginia.
2001
VIB opens a Base Supply Center in the Presidential Towers building in Crystal City, Virginia.
2002
VIB opens five Base Supply Centers:
  • Hoffman Building, Alexandria, Virginia
  • AMC Building, Crystal City, Virginia
  • Pentagon, Arlington, Virginia
  • Fort Lee, Petersburg, Virginia
  • Jefferson Plaza, Crystal City, Virginia
2003
VIB opens a Base Supply Center (Touch-n-Go ServMart) at Oceana Naval Air Station, Virginia Beach, Virginia
2004
Switchboard operation contract begins at the Veterans Administration Medical Center, Salem, Virginia.
2005
VIB opens a Base Supply Center on Fort Eustis, Newport News, Virginia.
2008
VIB opens a Base Supply Center on Fort Belvoir, Alexandria, Virginia.
2009
VIB opens a Base Supply Center on the Defense Supply Center in Richmond, Virginia
Committee for Purchase changes the name of the program from the Javits-Wagner-O'Day Program (JWOD) to the AbilityOne program. The name and purpose of the act remain unchanged.
2010
VIB begins providing Contract Management Support (CMS) services on a contract for Department of Defense and Federal agencies for Contract Close-out services.
2011
VIB opens a Base Supply Center in the Mark Center Federal Building, Alexandria, Virginia.
2012
VIB opens a Base Supply Center in the Defense Health Headquarters building in Falls Church, Virginia.
2013
VIB is awarded two AbilityOne Procurement List assignments to provide multiple kitting services.
VIB opens a Base Supply Center in the Taylor Building, Crystal City, Virginia.
